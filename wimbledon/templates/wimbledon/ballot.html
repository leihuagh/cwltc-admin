Saturday 21 April, 5pm

This year's Wimbledon ballot is different from recent years. There will be no signup sheet on the noticeboard. Instead, the draw will be made in an event open to all members on Saturday 21 April, starting at 5pm in the clubhouse.

We have 37 pairs of tickets spread across all 13 days of the tournament - 14 pairs for centre court, 16 pairs for court 1 and 7 pairs for court 2. The price per pair ranges from £60 to £420.

Eligibility

You are eligible if you are an adult or junior member of the club, hold an active Lite or Team British Tennis Membership and opted in to the Wimbledon ballot on the LTA site before the deadline in February.

The LTA ballot rules state that all opted-in members must have an equal chance of winning a ticket whether they attend the event or not. This will be the case in our ballot, but if you attend you will have more choice over which tickets you are allocated if you win.

The ballot process

The names of all eligible members will go into a hat and will be drawn in turn. If your name is drawn and you are present at the event, you can choose a pair of tickets from any that remain unallocated.

If you are not present at the event, you have 3 options:
  1. You can appoint a proxy who can choose tickets on your behalf.
  2. You can use the button at the top of this page to specify any tickets/dates that you do not want.
  3. You can simply do nothing.

In cases 2 and 3, you will be allocated the next available ticket from a predefined sequence, ignoring any that you have said that you do not want. If there are no remaining tickets that meet your requirements you will go on a standby list for tickets that are returned.

If you do not want any tickets and opted in solely to increase our allocation please use the opt out button. You can quickly opt out of all dates.

Purchasing tickets

If you are allocated a pair of tickets you will receive an email from the LTA and will have 14 days to pay for them. Payment is made direct to the LTA. If you reject the tickets or do not pay for them in time, they will be returned to the club and will be allocated to someone on the standby list or redrawn.

Conditions

You must use any tickets that you win for yourself with a guest of your choosing, who does not have to be a member of the club. If any tickets are won by a junior under the age of 16 they must attend the Championships with an adult and be supervised appropriately. Under no circumstances can you sell the tickets.

Parking

The ballot event on 21 April is likely to be busy and, bearing in mind it will take place at the end of social tennis, the car park will inevitably be full. Please share cars, cycle, walk or use public transport as far as possible.
